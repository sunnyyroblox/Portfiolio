Movie Project

The Shawshank Redemption (1994)

Directed by Frank Darabont, this film is based on a novella by Stephen King. It tells the story of Andy Dufresne, a banker wrongfully imprisoned for the murder of his wife and her lover. Throughout his time at Shawshank State Penitentiary, he forms an unlikely friendship with fellow inmate Ellis "Red" Redding and finds a way to triumph over adversity.

Forrest Gump (1994)

Directed by Robert Zemeckis, this film stars Tom Hanks as Forrest Gump, a man with a low IQ who leads an extraordinary life. Despite his intellectual challenges, Forrest influences several historical events and has profound impacts on those around him, all while remaining devoted to his childhood love, Jenny.

The Godfather (1972)

Directed by Francis Ford Coppola, this iconic film is a saga of the powerful Corleone crime family. With Marlon Brando as Vito Corleone and Al Pacino as his son Michael, the film explores themes of power, loyalty, and family in the world of organized crime.

Pulp Fiction (1994)

Directed by Quentin Tarantino, this film is known for its eclectic dialogue and nonlinear narrative. It intertwines multiple storylines involving crime and redemption in Los Angeles, featuring an ensemble cast including John Travolta, Uma Thurman, and Samuel L. Jackson.
